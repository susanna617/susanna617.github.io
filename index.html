NoteIt: The "Brain Dump" Browser Extension for Your Various Needs

Experimented with browser extension capabilities to create an extension that allows users to stay immersed in their work. Prevents the need for frantic tab-switching or external software, and allows the user to simply have one window open. Started from need-finding to define a solveable problem, and ended with demos and user testing to figure out where to take the extension in the future. Created with html/css/javascript.

A Race Against the Clock: Evaluating the Effectiveness of Gestural Inputs vs. Keyboard Inputs in Time Freeze

We performed a case study on our custom hand gesture control scheme in comparison to a video game's original keyboard inputs. Collected user qualitative and quantitative data, then used data visualization with Altair in Python to analyze the effectiveness of our design, based on the time efficiency, cognitive workload, and overall enjoyment of each tested user.

Hands on the Clock: Designing Gesture-Based Inputs for Time Freeze

Designed a control scheme for Time Freeze, a web game from Armour Games, by replacing the default keyboard inputs with custom hand gesture controls recognized by the user's monitor camera. We utilized the MediaPipe Gesture Recognizer library with Python to recognize the gestures. The project involved a heavy revision process for which hand gestures were most recognizable, and most comfortable for the user.

Led Astray: A Reflection on Finding and Creating Routes on Outdoor Fitness Apps

A project focused solely on the needfinding process. I interviewed various people on their experiences with trail-finding and trail-mapping technologies to discover what current problems existed, then used empathy maps and a user-centered approach to discover the frustrations and limitations of current trail-planning technologies.
